Title: Iran’s War With Israel and the United States

On February 28, the United States and Israel [attacked Iran](https://www.cfr.org/articles/gauging-the-impact-of-massive-u-s-israeli-strikes-on-iran) after weeks of military buildup and threats from President Trump. Large-scale strikes targeted Iranian military assets and the Islamic Republic’s top leadership, killing Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei. Tehran’s Assembly of Experts appointed Ali Khamenei’s son, [Mojtaba Khamenei](https://www.cfr.org/articles/islamic-republics-power-centers), to succeed him. Iran has retaliated by targeting U.S. military facilities, as well as energy and civilian infrastructure in the Gulf states. Israel has also stepped up its air strikes in Lebanon after [Hezbollah](https://www.cfr.org/backgrounders/what-hezbollah) fired rockets into Israel in support of Iran. More than [1,800 people](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/10/us/politics/trump-civilian-deaths-iran-war.html) have been killed so far, including 8 U.S. service members and at least 175 students who were killed by a [reported U.S. strike](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/03/08/world/middleeast/iran-minab-school-strike.html) on an Iranian elementary school. Trump has issued conflicting statements on U.S. military objectives and the trajectory of the conflict.

The attack quickly escalated into a regional war with widespread ramifications for [critical supply chains](https://www.cfr.org/articles/the-iran-wars-hidden-front-food-water-and-fertilizer) and [humanitarian aid](https://www.cfr.org/articles/the-iran-war-is-breaking-global-humanitarian-aid-efforts). Iran’s effective closure of the [Strait of Hormuz](https://www.cfr.org/articles/strait-hormuz-us-iran-maritime-flash-point) has caused a global energy shock, prompting the International Energy Agency to release four hundred million barrels from its strategic reserve.

## History of Iran’s Nuclear Program

Iran has pursued a nuclear program since at least 1957, with varying degrees of success. During a war with Iraq, Iran decided to develop nuclear weapons to ensure its security in the late 1980s. Consequently, Iran pursued agreements with China and Russia to support the program’s research throughout the 1990s. In the summer of 2002, the National Council of Resistance of Iran, an umbrella organization made up of Iranian dissident groups, [](https://carnegieendowment.org/2012/12/06/international-atomic-energy-agency-s-decision-to-find-iran-in-non-compliance-2002-2006-pub-50515)[exposed the existence](https://carnegieendowment.org/2012/12/06/international-atomic-energy-agency-s-decision-to-find-iran-in-non-compliance-2002-2006-pub-50515) of two Iranian nuclear sites that were hidden from the IAEA.

By 2003, diplomats launched an [](https://www.armscontrol.org/factsheets/Timeline-of-Nuclear-Diplomacy-With-Iran#2003)[intensive effort](https://www.armscontrol.org/factsheets/Timeline-of-Nuclear-Diplomacy-With-Iran#2003) to halt Iran’s nuclear program. Iran agreed, insisting only on keeping its centrifuges for nuclear energy. However, it did not follow through on its commitment to transparent reporting to the IAEA and continued covert activities, leading to a [](https://www.washingtonpost.com/archive/politics/2004/06/19/un-agency-rebukes-iran-on-nuclear-activity/29668d20-db20-4663-a5b4-ba94ab0ccadf/)[June 2004 rebuke](https://www.washingtonpost.com/archive/politics/2004/06/19/un-agency-rebukes-iran-on-nuclear-activity/29668d20-db20-4663-a5b4-ba94ab0ccadf/) and a [](https://www.nti.org/analysis/articles/new-iaea-resolution/)[September 2005 finding](https://www.nti.org/analysis/articles/new-iaea-resolution/) of non-compliance by the IAEA, paving the way for a future referral to the UN Security Council (UNSC). In 2006, the UNSC adopted [](https://carnegieendowment.org/2006/08/21/un-resolution-1696-moots-iranian-legal-claims-pub-18636)[Resolution 1696](https://carnegieendowment.org/2006/08/21/un-resolution-1696-moots-iranian-legal-claims-pub-18636), the first legally binding call for Iran to suspend its uranium enrichment program. Over the next few years, the UNSC adopted a series of resolutions imposing crippling economic sanctions on Iran for its failure to suspend its enrichment-related activities.

Between 2011 and 2015, the compounding effects of international sanctions led Iran’s [](https://sgp.fas.org/crs/mideast/RS20871.pdf)[economy to contract](https://sgp.fas.org/crs/mideast/RS20871.pdf) by 20 percent and [unemployment to rise](https://www.worldbank.org/en/country/iran/overview) to 20 percent. In 2013, Hassan Rouhani, a noted pragmatist, won Iran’s presidential election, campaigning on a promise to [](https://www.nytimes.com/2013/09/20/world/middleeast/iran-said-to-seek-a-nuclear-accord-to-end-sanctions.html)[lift sanctions](https://www.nytimes.com/2013/09/20/world/middleeast/iran-said-to-seek-a-nuclear-accord-to-end-sanctions.html) and restore the economy. Over the next two years, the United States convened several rounds of bilateral talks and led the other P5+1 coalition members—China, France, Germany, Russia, and the United Kingdom—in negotiations with Iran’s new leadership. These efforts culminated in the adoption of the [Joint Comprehensive Plan of Action](https://www.cfr.org/backgrounder/what-iran-nuclear-deal) ([](https://www.cfr.org/backgrounder/what-iran-nuclear-deal)JCPOA) in 2015. Once key parties signed the agreement, the UNSC approved [](https://www.un.org/securitycouncil/content/2231/background)[UN Resolution 2231](https://www.un.org/securitycouncil/content/2231/background), paving the way for sanctions relief.

The JCPOA required Iran to [](https://www.armscontrol.org/factsheets/JCPOA-at-a-glance)[reduce its stockpile](https://www.armscontrol.org/factsheets/JCPOA-at-a-glance) of enriched uranium by 98 percent for fifteen years, cut the number of operating centrifuges by two-thirds for ten years, and provide inspectors [](https://www.bbc.com/news/world-middle-east-33521655)[access to enrichment facilities](https://www.bbc.com/news/world-middle-east-33521655) within twenty-four days if the IAEA suspects violations. Moreover, if the IAEA confirmed violations, the JCPOA allowed for the immediate reinstatement of sanctions. After the JCPOA entered into force on January 16, 2016, Iran received [](https://www.nytimes.com/2016/01/17/world/middleeast/iran-sanctions-lifted-nuclear-deal.html)[sanctions relief](https://www.nytimes.com/2016/01/17/world/middleeast/iran-sanctions-lifted-nuclear-deal.html) totaling nearly $100 billion. However, Iran continued to [](https://missilethreat.csis.org/country/iran/)[develop ballistic missiles](https://missilethreat.csis.org/country/iran/), which, according to the United States, violated UN Resolution 2231.

## [](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMingFBVV95cUxQMWduRExjZXhMUjFuYjV4Zzc0LS11OHVKUEhXSlpUNUdFMm9MVGlJeFd5eGh1ZWpEWWhFNEdJX0tOVTlJbnZ5NmpBQ3dYeGNpSTBOSWtISUduak5WSHhfQUhQcHBKbVpLdG1IVi1ySWVGd05NM18yektrem1TM3ZvaV9xVjR2eC04cE9RZ3FZajNCZWFHNzBaS0hhSS02dw?oc=5)Iran’s Regional Proxies

Though the JCPOA limited Iran’s nuclear ambitions, its regional ambitions continued to grow. Iran persisted in [](https://www.csis.org/war-by-proxy)[arming and training](https://www.csis.org/war-by-proxy) Shiite militants through its [](https://carnegieendowment.org/2018/01/30/tehran-s-shia-foreign-legions-pub-75387)[Quds Force](https://carnegieendowment.org/2018/01/30/tehran-s-shia-foreign-legions-pub-75387)—the international arm of the [Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps](https://www.cfr.org/backgrounder/irans-revolutionary-guards) (IRGC)—which has exacerbated sectarian divisions in the Middle East. Iran has provided years of [military aid and training](https://www.washingtoninstitute.org/policy-analysis/hamas-iran-relationship) to the Palestinian militant group Hamas, which enabled its October 7, 2023, attack on Israel. The Quds Force has also provided [](https://www.theatlantic.com/international/archive/2018/07/hezbollah-iran-new-weapons-israel/565796/)[advanced armed drones](https://www.theatlantic.com/international/archive/2018/07/hezbollah-iran-new-weapons-israel/565796/) to Hezbollah in Lebanon, trained and funded more than [](https://www.csis.org/war-by-proxy)[one hundred thousand](https://www.csis.org/war-by-proxy) Shiite fighters in Syria, supplied [](https://www.reuters.com/article/us-iran-sanctions-un/parts-of-missiles-fired-at-saudi-arabia-came-from-iran-u-n-chief-idUSKBN1JA34F)[ballistic missiles](https://www.reuters.com/article/us-iran-sanctions-un/parts-of-missiles-fired-at-saudi-arabia-came-from-iran-u-n-chief-idUSKBN1JA34F) and [](https://www.apnews.com/02c0c90050ee4b21a408db483906971b)[drones](https://www.apnews.com/02c0c90050ee4b21a408db483906971b) to Yemen’s Houthis, and helped Shiite militias in Iraq [](https://www.reuters.com/article/us-iran-iraq-missiles-exclusive/exclusive-iran-moves-missiles-to-iraq-in-warning-to-enemies-idUSKCN1LG0WB)[build missile capabilities](https://www.reuters.com/article/us-iran-iraq-missiles-exclusive/exclusive-iran-moves-missiles-to-iraq-in-warning-to-enemies-idUSKCN1LG0WB).

The U.S. government considers Iran to be the [](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/iran-still-top-state-sponsor-terrorism-u-s-report-says)[foremost state sponsor of terrorism](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/iran-still-top-state-sponsor-terrorism-u-s-report-says), spending more than one billion dollars on terrorist financing annually. There are between [](https://www.csis.org/war-by-proxy)[140,000 and 185,000](https://www.csis.org/war-by-proxy) IRGC-Quds Force partner forces across Afghanistan, Gaza, Lebanon, Pakistan, Syria, and Yemen.

## [](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMingFBVV95cUxQMWduRExjZXhMUjFuYjV4Zzc0LS11OHVKUEhXSlpUNUdFMm9MVGlJeFd5eGh1ZWpEWWhFNEdJX0tOVTlJbnZ5NmpBQ3dYeGNpSTBOSWtISUduak5WSHhfQUhQcHBKbVpLdG1IVi1ySWVGd05NM18yektrem1TM3ZvaV9xVjR2eC04cE9RZ3FZajNCZWFHNzBaS0hhSS02dw?oc=5)Trump’s First-Term Clash With Iran

Because the JCPOA only addressed Iran’s nuclear program—and not its revisionism or ballistic missile programs—the first Trump administration [](https://www.nytimes.com/2018/05/08/world/middleeast/trump-iran-nuclear-deal.html)[withdrew the United States](https://www.nytimes.com/2018/05/08/world/middleeast/trump-iran-nuclear-deal.html) from the agreement, pledging to seek a more comprehensive deal. In 2018, the Trump administration began [](https://www.state.gov/a-force-for-good-america-reinvigorated-in-the-middle-east/)[reimposing sanctions](https://www.state.gov/a-force-for-good-america-reinvigorated-in-the-middle-east/) on Iran and demanded that European countries withdraw from the JCPOA as part of a new containment strategy. U.S. sanctions sparked the [](https://www.nytimes.com/2019/01/30/world/middleeast/iran-economy.html?searchResultPosition=109)[worst economic crisis](https://www.nytimes.com/2019/01/30/world/middleeast/iran-economy.html?searchResultPosition=109) Iran has faced in forty years, [cutting Iranian oil exports](https://www.reuters.com/article/iran-oil-exports/irans-may-crude-exports-slide-to-400000-bpd-data-sources-idUSL8N234504) by more than half and [](https://www.nytimes.com/2019/06/15/world/middleeast/trump-iran-hard-liners.html)[emboldening Iranian hardliners](https://www.nytimes.com/2019/06/15/world/middleeast/trump-iran-hard-liners.html).

While the Trump administration pursued a strategy of maximum pressure to bring Iran to the [](https://www.nytimes.com/2018/07/30/us/politics/trump-iran-rouhani.html)[negotiating table](https://www.nytimes.com/2018/07/30/us/politics/trump-iran-rouhani.html), Iran began to contravene the JCPOA’s restrictions on its nuclear program, raising tensions. In April 2019, the United States designated the IRGC a terrorist organization. When the Trump administration received intelligence of potential Iranian attacks on U.S. troops, it deployed bombers, carriers, and additional forces to the Middle East. Over the next month, [](https://www.cnbc.com/2019/06/22/oil-tanker-attacks-in-the-strait-of-hormuz-requires-an-international-response-us-envoy-to-iran-says.html)[six oil tankers](https://www.cnbc.com/2019/06/22/oil-tanker-attacks-in-the-strait-of-hormuz-requires-an-international-response-us-envoy-to-iran-says.html) in or near the Strait of Hormuz were attacked, which U.S. government officials blamed on Iran.

In late June 2019, Iran [](https://www.nytimes.com/2019/06/20/us/politics/drone-shot-down-iran-us.html)[downed](https://www.nytimes.com/2019/06/20/us/politics/drone-shot-down-iran-us.html) a U.S. [](https://www.reuters.com/article/us-mideast-iran-usa-factbox/factbox-the-global-hawk-drone-shot-down-by-iran-idUSKCN1TL29K)[Global Hawk drone](https://www.reuters.com/article/us-mideast-iran-usa-factbox/factbox-the-global-hawk-drone-shot-down-by-iran-idUSKCN1TL29K) in the Strait of Hormuz; President Trump ordered a [](https://www.nytimes.com/2019/06/22/us/politics/us-iran-cyber-attacks.html)[cyberattack](https://www.nytimes.com/2019/06/22/us/politics/us-iran-cyber-attacks.html) and the imposition of [](https://www.nytimes.com/2019/06/24/us/politics/iran-sanctions.html)[new sanctions](https://www.nytimes.com/2019/06/24/us/politics/iran-sanctions.html) in response. On December 31, Trump blamed Iran for backing protests that tried to seize the U.S. Embassy in Baghdad. Days later, tensions peaked when the United States killed [](https://www.cfr.org/in-brief/did-soleimani-pose-imminent-threat)[Qasem Soleimani](https://www.cfr.org/in-brief/did-soleimani-pose-imminent-threat), the commander of Iran’s Quds Force, in a Baghdad air strike. In response, Iran said it would no longer adhere to [](https://www.nytimes.com/2020/01/05/world/middleeast/iran-general-soleimani-iraq.html)[restrictions](https://www.nytimes.com/2020/01/05/world/middleeast/iran-general-soleimani-iraq.html) under the nuclear deal, and it accidentally [](https://www.reuters.com/article/us-iran-crash-ukraine/irans-final-report-blames-air-defence-operator-error-for-ukraine-plane-crash-idUSKBN2B92CL)[shot down](https://www.reuters.com/article/us-iran-crash-ukraine/irans-final-report-blames-air-defence-operator-error-for-ukraine-plane-crash-idUSKBN2B92CL) a Ukrainian passenger plane while on high alert. In late 2020, Trump continued to ratchet up [](https://iranprimer.usip.org/blog/2020/dec/16/us-sanctions-iran-2020#:~:text=In%202020%2C%20the%20United%20States,and%20support%20for%20extremist%20movements.)[sanctions](https://iranprimer.usip.org/blog/2020/dec/16/us-sanctions-iran-2020#:~:text=In%202020%2C%20the%20United%20States,and%20support%20for%20extremist%20movements.), and Iran [](https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/middle_east/iran-nuclear-uranium-enrichment/2021/01/04/588949be-4e76-11eb-a1f5-fdaf28cfca90_story.html)[boosted](https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/middle_east/iran-nuclear-uranium-enrichment/2021/01/04/588949be-4e76-11eb-a1f5-fdaf28cfca90_story.html) uranium enrichment to levels well beyond the limits of the nuclear deal after one of its top nuclear scientists was killed.

## [](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMingFBVV95cUxQMWduRExjZXhMUjFuYjV4Zzc0LS11OHVKUEhXSlpUNUdFMm9MVGlJeFd5eGh1ZWpEWWhFNEdJX0tOVTlJbnZ5NmpBQ3dYeGNpSTBOSWtISUduak5WSHhfQUhQcHBKbVpLdG1IVi1ySWVGd05NM18yektrem1TM3ZvaV9xVjR2eC04cE9RZ3FZajNCZWFHNzBaS0hhSS02dw?oc=5)Conflict Between Israel and Iran

The outbreak of war between Israel, a close U.S. ally, and the Palestinian Iran-backed militant group Hamas in October 2023 escalated tensions between Iran and Israel. Iran-backed proxy forces ramped up strikes in protest of Israel’s military incursion into the Gaza Strip, including more than [](https://www.washingtoninstitute.org/policy-analysis/tracking-anti-us-and-anti-israel-strikes-iraq-and-syria-during-gaza-crisis)[two hundred attacks](https://www.washingtoninstitute.org/policy-analysis/tracking-anti-us-and-anti-israel-strikes-iraq-and-syria-during-gaza-crisis) on U.S. and Israeli targets in Iraq and Syria. In response, the United States ordered air strikes on two Iran-backed facilities on October 26, 2023, and eighty-five more Iran-affiliated targets in the two countries on February 2, 2024. The [](https://www.cfr.org/global-conflict-tracker/conflict/war-yemen)[Houthis in Yemen](https://www.cfr.org/global-conflict-tracker/conflict/war-yemen) and [](https://www.cfr.org/global-conflict-tracker/conflict/political-instability-lebanon)[Hezbollah in Lebanon](https://www.cfr.org/global-conflict-tracker/conflict/political-instability-lebanon)—both actors in Iran’s axis of resistance—also launched attacks from the Red Sea and Israel’s northern border with Lebanon, spurring fears of regional spillover.

In 2024, Israel and Iran’s confrontation shifted from indirect, proxy-based hostilities to direct exchanges of strikes. On April 1, a suspected [](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/iran-says-it-will-retaliate-israels-attack-its-damascus-consulate-2024-04-02/)[Israeli air strike](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/iran-says-it-will-retaliate-israels-attack-its-damascus-consulate-2024-04-02/) against an Iranian consular building in Damascus, Syria, killed two of its generals and five military advisors. Iran retaliated by launching over three hundred [](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/4/13/israeli-army-says-iran-has-launched-drones-at-israel)[drone and missile attacks](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/4/13/israeli-army-says-iran-has-launched-drones-at-israel), the first time Iran had [](https://apnews.com/article/israel-iran-hamas-latest-04-15-2024-05ca1ac2780defa8a1a0345754708512)[directly targeted](https://apnews.com/article/israel-iran-hamas-latest-04-15-2024-05ca1ac2780defa8a1a0345754708512) Israel.

Following Israel’s killing of the leaders of Hamas and Hezbollah, Iran launched [](https://www.cnn.com/world/live-news/israel-lebanon-war-hezbollah-10-1-24-intl-hnk)[180 ballistic missiles](https://www.cnn.com/world/live-news/israel-lebanon-war-hezbollah-10-1-24-intl-hnk) against Israel in October 2024. Israel then launched its [](https://www.wsj.com/world/middle-east/how-israel-pulled-off-its-largest-ever-strike-on-iran-689022ca)[largest direct attack](https://www.wsj.com/world/middle-east/how-israel-pulled-off-its-largest-ever-strike-on-iran-689022ca) on Iran, targeting its air defenses and missile production facilities. Israel’s [](https://www.axios.com/2024/10/20/israel-assassinations-sinwar-hamas-hezbollah)decimation of [Hamas and Hezbollah leadership](https://www.axios.com/2024/10/20/israel-assassinations-sinwar-hamas-hezbollah), coupled with the downfall of the Bashar al-Assad regime in Syria, considerably weakened Iran’s axis of resistance in 2024.

Upon returning to office in 2025, U.S. President Donald Trump restored his maximum pressure campaign against Tehran while also initiating negotiations on its nuclear program—the [](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4g2eggzvjgo)[first direct U.S.-Iran talks](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c4g2eggzvjgo) since he withdrew the United States from the JCPOA nuclear deal in 2018. Israel was wholly opposed to the negotiations and has maintained an unwavering commitment to dismantling Iran’s nuclear program. Israeli officials argue that Iran’s clandestine efforts to develop nuclear weapons would fundamentally alter the regional balance of power, posing a [direct danger](https://www.axios.com/2025/06/13/how-israel-executed-strike-iran-nuclear) to Israel’s survival.

On June 12, the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) declared Iran was violating its [non-proliferation obligations](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/ce3v6w2qr12o) for the first time in twenty years, prompting Iran to announce it would open a secret uranium enrichment site. The next day, Israel launched a [](https://www.nytimes.com/live/2025/06/12/world/israel-iran-us-nuclear)[unilateral military strike](https://www.nytimes.com/live/2025/06/12/world/israel-iran-us-nuclear) against Iran, targeting nuclear facilities, missile factories, senior military officials, and nuclear scientists. Iran’s Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi [](https://www.barrons.com/news/iran-says-israel-attack-declaration-of-war-69f8886e)[declared the attack](https://www.barrons.com/news/iran-says-israel-attack-declaration-of-war-69f8886e) “an act of war,” and Iran retaliated by launching waves of drones and dozens of ballistic missiles. Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu described the operation as a last-resort effort to prevent Iran from acquiring nuclear weapons. Although the Trump administration had recently [](https://www.cfr.org/expert-brief/where-us-iran-nuclear-talks-are-headed)[resumed nuclear negotiations](https://www.cfr.org/expert-brief/where-us-iran-nuclear-talks-are-headed), President Trump increasingly [](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/israel-iran-conflict-news)[voiced support](https://www.wsj.com/livecoverage/israel-iran-conflict-news) for Israel’s objectives and signaled his openness to [regime change](https://www.axios.com/2025/06/22/trump-iran-regime-change) in Tehran.

Following a week of air strikes between Israel and Iran, the United States [directly intervened](https://www.cfr.org/articles/us-israel-attack-iranian-nuclear-targets-damage-so-far) in the conflict, attacking three Iranian nuclear sites in Fordow, Isfahan, and Natanz on June 21. The Trump administration claimed the strikes [significantly hindered](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2025/jun/29/iran-will-likely-be-able-to-produce-enriched-uranium-in-a-matter-of-months-iaea-chief-says) Iran’s capacity to achieve weapons-grade uranium, but the head of the UN nuclear watchdog assessed the program was set back by a matter of months. Trump is the [first U.S. president](https://www.economist.com/middle-east-and-africa/2025/06/22/mission-accomplished-for-netanyahu) to attack another country’s nuclear program and the first to explicitly join Israel in an attack on an adversary. Iran retaliated on June 23, launching a [missile attack on U.S. forces](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/iran-weighs-retaliation-against-us-strikes-nuclear-sites-2025-06-23/) stationed at the Al Udeid Air Base in Qatar; no casualties were reported. Trump announced a ceasefire later that day. Although both sides accused the other of [continuing strikes](https://www.nytimes.com/live/2025/06/24/world/ceasefire-iran-israel-trump), the truce has largely held.

##### Iranian Authorities Cut Internet Access Nationwide

January 9, 2026

Iranian authorities have struggled to contain antigovernment protests that have swelled across the country [(_NYT_)](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/01/08/world/middleeast/iran-protests-internet-shutdown.html). Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei said today that authorities would “not back down” in the face of protests [(_NYT_)](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/01/09/world/middleeast/iran-protests-ayatollah-khamenei.html). Khamenei has accused demonstrators of acting on behalf of Trump[(NBC)](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/09/iran-protests-supreme-leader-khamenei-trump-oil.html). Iranian opposition figure Reza Pahlavi, son of the shah ousted in the 1979 Islamic Revolution, called for nationwide demonstrations last night and tonight [(X)](https://x.com/PahlaviReza/status/2009499835368460485).

##### Trump Warns of U.S. Consequences If Iranian Authorities Kill demonstrators

January 8, 2026

Iranian security forces’ efforts to repress the demonstrations have not stopped their growth; they have now entered their twelfth day [(BBC;](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cgm4y0ewe93o)[HRANA)](https://www.en-hrana.org/from-the-streets-to-universities-irans-protests-enter-their-eleventh-day/). U.S.-based Human Rights Activists News Agency said that more than forty-two people have died and more than two thousand have been detained since demonstrations began on December 28 [(HRANA)](https://www.en-hrana.org/a-report-on-the-twelfth-day-of-nationwide-protests-in-iran-widespread-strikes-internet-shutdown-and-surge-in-arrests/). Protesters are denouncing both economic hardship—the country’s currency collapsed late last month—and political repression, with many openly calling for regime change [(Bloomberg)](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-01-08/iran-protests-explained-how-inflation-and-a-currency-crash-fueled-unrest). Reformist President Masoud Pezeshkian’s administration has sought to engage with protesters and introduce economic stipends and other concessions, but Iranians have stayed on the streets [(_FT_)](https://www.ft.com/content/0e1762e0-8aa6-44b5-977c-0865ecdcbb7d). Trump told the “Hugh Hewitt Show” that Washington was watching the demonstrations “very closely;” he said Iranian authorities have “been told very strongly” that they will “pay hell” if they kill demonstrators, though he characterized the deaths so far as consequences of crowd control problems [(Hugh Hewitt)](https://hughhewitt.com/president-trump-returns-to-the-hugh-hewitt-show). In a separate interview that aired on Fox News, Trump alleged that Khamenei could be looking to leave Iran [(Fox News)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V0dDaAg3-FI). Iraq-based Kurdish opposition parties are calling for a general strike in support of the protest movement that has spread to the Kurdish regions of western Iran [(France24)](https://www.france24.com/en/asia-pacific/20260108-iranians-protest-for-12th-day-as-kurdish-opposition-calls-for-general-strike).

##### Iran Responds to Protests

January 5, 2026

The government announced a plan to give most citizens monthly payments of about $7 in response to widespread demonstrations fueled by economic hardship [(_NYT_)](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/01/05/world/europe/iran-protests-payments.html). The protests have become the largest in the country since 2022, when the death of Mahsa Amini in police custody sparked months of civic upheaval [(AP)](https://apnews.com/article/iran-protests-us-israel-nuclear-economy-cf491782ecafdc9098878e5a4e5dc384).

##### Iran’s Protests Spread

January 4, 2026

Antigovernment protests and labor strikes have spread to twenty-six provinces in Iran, U.S.-based group Human Rights Activists News Agency said; at least nineteen protesters have reportedly been killed as a result of the law enforcement crackdown [(HRANA)](https://www.en-hrana.org/detailed-report-on-the-eighth-day-of-protests-222-locations-across-the-country-witness-demonstrations/). After Iran’s top leader said Saturday that “rioters must be put in their place,” Trump told reporters that if Iran starts “killing people like they have in the past, I think they’re going to get hit very hard by the United States” [(AP;](https://apnews.com/article/iran-protests-nuclear-us-what-to-know-explainer-845b3ac10c37727add7118ec9c2f6e46)[White House)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9XVjd3R2T3g).
