Tennessee Wesleyan University is set to hold a lecture on the current US-Iran conflict this week.

TWU Assistant Professor of History Dr. John B. Seitz has announced he will host a lecture examining the evolving conflict between the United States and Iran on March 26 at 6:30 p.m., placing current events within a broader historical and geopolitical context.

The event will take place in the Glenn Lowe Room, located in Sherman Hall on the Tennessee Wesleyan University campus and is open to members from the community.

Titled “Iran and the United States: The Current Conflict, Historical Continuities, and Global Implications,” the lecture will explore the latest developments in the region, including recent military actions and analyze their potential impact on global energy systems, agriculture and U.S. alliances.

Seitz specializes in environmental history, the history of technology and the study of empire in Islamic Central Asia, with a particular focus on energy and agriculture.

This lecture offers a timely opportunity for students and community members to better understand the historical roots and global consequences of one of today’s most significant international conflicts.
